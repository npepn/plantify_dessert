## Component Breakdown

### Component 1: Choux Pastry Shell (40% of dessert)

**Required Functions**:
- Fat structuring (for lamination and texture)
- Foaming (for aeration and puff)
- Binding (for structure)
- Moisture retention (for steam generation)

**Ingredients**:
- Water: 250ml
- Plant-Based Butter: 100g
- All-Purpose Flour: 150g
- Aquafaba: 200ml
- Fine Sea Salt: 2g

**Why These Ingredients?**

1. **Water (250ml)**
   - Function: Moisture for steam generation
   - Critical for puff: Steam creates hollow interior
   - Must reach boiling point (100°C) for proper expansion

2. **Plant-Based Butter (100g)**
   - Function: Fat structuring, flavor carrier
   - Replaces: Traditional butter
   - Properties: 80% fat content, melting point 32°C
   - Why chosen: Emulsifiers in vegan butter help bind water and fat
   - CO₂ impact: 1.8 kg/kg (60% lower than dairy butter)

3. **All-Purpose Flour (150g)**
   - Function: Binding, structure
   - Properties: 10.5% protein (gluten formation)
   - Critical: Gluten network traps steam for puff
   - Ratio: 60% hydration (optimal for choux)

4. **Aquafaba (200ml)**
   - Function: Foaming, emulsification, binding
   - Replaces: 4-5 eggs
   - Properties: 0.5% protein, 85% foaming capacity
   - Why chosen: Creates structure without eggs, whips to incorporate air
   - CO₂ impact: 0.3 kg/kg (95% lower than eggs)
   - Cost: €0.50/kg (90% cheaper than eggs)

5. **Fine Sea Salt (2g)**
   - Function: Flavor enhancement
   - Strengthens gluten network

**Critical Properties Achieved**:
- Fat content: 18% ✓ (target: 15-25%)
- Water content: 55% ✓ (target: 50-60%)
- Protein content: 9% ✓ (target: 8-12%)

**Ingredients**:
- Coconut Cream: 400ml
- Organic Cane Sugar: 80g
- Cornstarch: 30g
- Pure Vanilla Extract: 10ml
- Fine Sea Salt: 1g

**Why These Ingredients?**

1. **Coconut Cream (400ml)**
   - Function: Emulsification, flavor carrier, fat source
   - Replaces: Heavy dairy cream
   - Properties: 24% fat, 68% water, viscosity 5000 cps
   - Why chosen: High fat content for richness, natural emulsifiers
   - CO₂ impact: 2.8 kg/kg vs 4.5 kg/kg for dairy cream (38% reduction)
   - Texture: Creamy, smooth mouthfeel

2. **Organic Cane Sugar (80g)**
   - Function: Sweetening, texture modification
   - Affects viscosity and mouthfeel
   - Prevents ice crystal formation if frozen

3. **Cornstarch (30g)**
   - Function: Thickening, binding
   - Replaces: Egg yolks (traditional thickener)
   - Properties: Creates clear, glossy gel when cooked
   - Ratio: 7.5% of liquid (optimal for pastry cream consistency)
   - Why chosen: Neutral flavor, reliable thickening, gluten-free
   - CO₂ impact: 0.7 kg/kg (very low)

4. **Pure Vanilla Extract (10ml)**
   - Function: Flavor carrier
   - Essential for classic éclair taste
   - Use real extract, not imitation

5. **Fine Sea Salt (1g)**
   - Function: Flavor enhancement
   - Balances sweetness

**Critical Properties Achieved**:
- Fat content: 12% ✓ (target: 8-15%)
- Viscosity: 8000 cps ✓ (target: 5000-15000 cps)
- Smooth, pipeable consistency

### Component 3: Chocolate Glaze (10% of dessert)

**Required Functions**:
- Fat structuring (for glossy finish)
- Crystallization (for proper set)
- Flavor carrier

**Ingredients**:
- Dutch-Process Cocoa Powder: 40g
- Refined Coconut Oil: 60g
- Pure Maple Syrup: 40ml

**Why These Ingredients?**

1. **Dutch-Process Cocoa Powder (40g)**
   - Function: Flavor, color
   - Properties: pH 7.0 (neutral), 10% fat
   - Why Dutch-process: Darker color, smoother flavor
   - Alkalized for better dispersion in fat

2. **Refined Coconut Oil (60g)**
   - Function: Fat structuring, glossy finish
   - Properties: Melting point 24°C, 100% fat
   - Why chosen: Sets at room temperature, creates shine
   - Refined = neutral flavor (no coconut taste)

3. **Pure Maple Syrup (40ml)**
   - Function: Sweetening, moisture
   - Adds depth of flavor
   - Helps glaze flow smoothly

**Critical Properties Achieved**:
- Fat content: 35% ✓ (target: 30-40%)
- Glossy, smooth finish
- Sets within 15 minutes at room temperature

## Complete Recipe Instructions

### Choux Pastry Shell

1. **Preheat** oven to 200°C (400°F). Line baking sheet with parchment paper.
   - *Critical*: Proper temperature essential for puff
   - Tip: Use oven thermometer to verify temperature

2. **Boil liquid and fat**: In medium saucepan, combine water, plant-based butter, and salt. Bring to rolling boil over medium-high heat.
   - *Critical*: Butter must be fully melted
   - Tip: Don't let water evaporate too much

3. **Add flour**: Remove from heat. Add all flour at once. Stir vigorously with wooden spoon until dough forms cohesive ball (30-60 seconds).
   - *Critical*: Mix quickly to prevent lumps
   - Tip: Dough should pull away from pan sides cleanly

4. **Dry the dough**: Return pan to medium heat. Cook 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until dough is slightly dried and film forms on bottom of pan.
   - *Critical*: This removes excess moisture for better puff
   - Tip: Dough should feel less sticky

5. **Cool**: Transfer dough to mixing bowl. Let cool 5 minutes to approximately 60°C.
   - *Critical*: Too hot will denature aquafaba proteins
   - Tip: Touch test - should be warm but not burning

6. **Add aquafaba**: Add aquafaba in 4 additions, mixing thoroughly after each. Dough should be smooth, glossy, and hold soft peaks when lifted.
   - *Critical*: Proper consistency is key
   - Tip: Final dough should slowly fall from spoon in thick ribbon

7. **Pipe**: Transfer to piping bag with 1.5cm round tip. Pipe 10cm logs onto prepared sheet, spacing 5cm apart.
   - Tip: Wet finger to smooth any peaks
   - Make uniform size for even baking

8. **Bake**: Bake 30 minutes at 200°C without opening oven. Reduce to 180°C, bake 10 more minutes until deep golden brown.
   - *Critical*: DO NOT open oven during first 30 minutes
   - Opening causes collapse due to steam loss
   - Shells should be firm and hollow-sounding when tapped

9. **Dry**: Turn off oven. Pierce each shell with knife tip. Leave in oven 10 minutes with door slightly ajar.
   - *Critical*: Prevents sogginess
   - Releases steam from interior

10. **Cool**: Remove from oven. Cool completely on wire rack before filling.

### Pastry Cream Filling

1. **Mix**: In medium saucepan, whisk together coconut cream, sugar, cornstarch, and salt until smooth with no lumps.
   - Tip: Whisk cornstarch thoroughly to prevent lumps

2. **Cook**: Place over medium heat. Cook, whisking constantly, until mixture thickens and bubbles, 5-7 minutes.
   - *Critical*: Constant whisking prevents scorching
   - Should coat back of spoon thickly
   - Temperature should reach 85-90°C

3. **Finish**: Remove from heat. Whisk in vanilla extract.

4. **Chill**: Transfer to bowl. Press plastic wrap directly onto surface (prevents skin). Refrigerate minimum 2 hours until cold and set.
   - Tip: Can be made day ahead

5. **Prepare for filling**: Before using, whisk cream until smooth and pipeable.

### Chocolate Glaze

1. **Melt**: In small bowl, melt coconut oil (microwave 20 seconds or double boiler).

2. **Mix**: Whisk in cocoa powder until smooth and no lumps remain.

3. **Sweeten**: Whisk in maple syrup until glossy and well combined.

4. **Use**: Use immediately while still fluid. Glaze will set as it cools.

### Assembly

1. **Slice**: Using serrated knife, carefully slice each cooled shell horizontally, creating top and bottom halves.

2. **Fill**: Transfer chilled pastry cream to piping bag with medium star tip. Pipe generous amount of cream into bottom half of each shell.
   - Tip: Fill generously but not overflowing

3. **Close**: Gently place top half of shell over cream.

4. **Glaze**: Dip top of each éclair into chocolate glaze, allowing excess to drip off.
   - Tip: Hold at 45° angle for even coating

5. **Set**: Place on wire rack. Let glaze set 15 minutes at room temperature.

6. **Serve**: Serve immediately or refrigerate up to 4 hours.

## Storage & Shelf Life

### Storage Instructions

- **Unfilled shells**: Airtight container at room temperature, up to 2 days
- **Filled éclairs**: Refrigerate, up to 1 day
- **Pastry cream**: Refrigerate covered, up to 3 days
- **Chocolate glaze**: Room temperature, up to 1 week

### Best Practices

- Fill shells just before serving for maximum crispness
- If shells soften, re-crisp in 180°C oven for 5 minutes
- Glaze can be reheated gently if it solidifies

## Professional Tips

1. **Aquafaba Quality**: Use liquid from canned chickpeas, not home-cooked (more consistent protein content)

2. **Oven Steam**: Place pan of water on bottom rack for extra steam during first 15 minutes

3. **Testing Doneness**: Shell should be deep golden and feel light/hollow when picked up

4. **Piping Consistency**: If dough is too thick, add aquafaba 1 tbsp at a time. Too thin, it won't hold shape.

5. **Make-Ahead**: Shells can be frozen unfilled for up to 1 month. Thaw and re-crisp before filling.
